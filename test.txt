Buying a self-help book on overcoming procrastination was a bold move, but it has been sitting on my nightstand for three months, serving as a very expensive coaster for my lukewarm tea. Every time I look at it, I feel a surge of productivity that lasts exactly as long as it takes to remember that there's a new season of that show about competitive competitive sandwich-making. I told myself I’d start Chapter One once I’d perfectly organized my sock drawer by thread count and emotional significance, but now I’m too busy researching if it’s possible to teach a goldfish to play the harmonica. I’m not saying I’m avoiding my responsibilities, but if there were an Olympic event for "Thinking About Doing Something While Actually Doing Nothing," I’d probably forget to show up for the medal ceremony.
